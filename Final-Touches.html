Stories

💜 Neon Cyberpunk Japan

⭐ What it is

A futuristic pixel world inspired by Tokyo — filled with neon lights, technology, rain, and mystery. This style mixes 90s pixel art with sci-fi cyberpunk vibes, where cities feel advanced but gritty, and tech blends into everyday life 🏙️✨.

🎮 Famous video game examples

  • Snatcher (1994) — cyberpunk mystery by Hideo Kojima 🕵️‍♂️🟦
  • Shadowrun (SNES) — neon, hacking, and dark city vibes 🌃💻
  • System Shock (PC) — retro-future technology and AI 🤖⚠️

❤️ Pixel Fighting Games & Arcades

⭐ What it is

A genre where players fight one-on-one using pixel characters, often inside Japanese arcades. These games created iconic animations, combos, and competitive culture 🥋🔥.

🎮 Famous video game examples

  • Street Fighter II — the blueprint of fighting games 🥷🔥
  • The King of Fighters ‘94 — iconic teams + pixel animation 👑⚔️
  • Samurai Shodown — sword duels + Japanese style 🗡️🌸

💖 Cozy Slice-of-Life Pixel Japan

The sound chips were the heart of 90s game music.

⭐ What it is

A calm pixel style inspired by daily life in Japan — ramen shops, cherry blossoms, and school days 🌸🍜🏮. Instead of action, it focuses on peace, comfort, and emotion.

🎮 Famous video game examples

  • Tokimeki Memorial (1994) — school memories and sweet moments 💕🎒
  • Harvest Moon (SNES, 1996) — farming, seasons, cozy routines 🌾🏡
  • Pokémon Yellow / Pocket Pikachu (1998) — cute pets, friendship, cozy adventures 🐭🌸
